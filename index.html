The 8-shot checklist

Work through these in every new environment. Get both vertical and horizontal for each.

01
Wide
Establish the place. Where are we?
02
Medium
Waist up, person in their environment
03
Close-up: face
Fill the frame. Eyes matter most.
04
Close-up: detail
Hands, objects, something being made
05
Over-the-shoulder
Behind someone, showing what they see
06
Low angle
Get down low, shoot up
07
Person in big space
Shows scale and context
08
Something unexpected
Reflection, shadow, or angle that caught your eye

Shoot vertical AND horizontal

Every shot, both orientations. It takes five extra seconds and doubles what you come home with.

Keep it still

Press your elbows into your body to brace your arms
Lean against a wall or post if one is nearby
If panning, move as slowly as you possibly can — then slower
For video, hold each clip for at least 10 seconds before stopping. Short clips can't be edited.
Film the same moment a few ways — wide, then medium, then close-up. Those three clips together tell a story.

Shoot more than you need

Expect only one or two in ten shots to be truly usable. That's completely normal — it's not failure, it's just how this works.

Be generous. Cover the scene from multiple angles, distances, and orientations. Give the editor options.

Quick reminders

Clean your lens before you start — a wipe makes a real difference

Light on the face, not behind it — avoid bright windows behind subjects

Get permission before filming individuals, especially children

Don't pinch to zoom — move closer or tap the lens button
